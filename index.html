The Luminous Sparks

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

This collection of speeches and writings by Dr. Kalam offers insights into his thoughts on leadership, education, and nation-building. With his characteristic optimism and wisdom, he inspires readers to strive for excellence, embrace challenges, and make a positive difference in the world.

The White Tiger

Aravind Adiga

Winner of the Booker Prize, this novel offers a darkly comic and provocative look at the underbelly of India's economic boom. Through the eyes of Balram Halwai, a chauffeur-turned-entrepreneur, Aravind Adiga explores themes of class struggle, corruption, and the pursuit of freedom.

To Kill a Mockingbird

Harper Lee

Set in the racially charged atmosphere of the American South during the 1930s, this novel follows the moral growth of young Scout Finch as she learns about empathy, justice, and the complexities of human nature through her father's defense of a black man falsely accused of raping a white woman.

1984

George Orwell

A dystopian classic depicting a totalitarian regime where individualism is suppressed, truth is manipulated, and surveillance is omnipresent. Winston Smith, the protagonist, rebels against the oppressive Party and its leader, Big Brother, in a world where even thoughts are controlled.

Pride and Prejudice

Jane Austen

This romantic comedy of manners follows the spirited Elizabeth Bennet as she navigates the societal expectations of early 19th-century England and confronts her own prejudices, ultimately finding love and self-awareness in the process.

The Great Gatsby

F. Scott Fitzgerald

Set in the Roaring Twenties, this novel explores the American Dream through the enigmatic Jay Gatsby, who throws extravagant parties to win back the love of his life, Daisy Buchanan, and exposes the moral decay and disillusionment lurking beneath the surface of wealth and glamour.

To the Lighthouse

Virginia Woolf

A modernist masterpiece that delves into the complexities of human relationships, memory, and the passage of time. Set on the Isle of Skye, the novel follows the Ramsay family and their guests as they grapple with their inner thoughts and desires during a visit to their summer home.

The Catcher in the Rye

J.D. Salinger

Narrated by the cynical and disillusioned teenager Holden Caulfield, this coming-of-age novel captures the angst and alienation of adolescence as Holden embarks on a journey of self-discovery through the streets of New York City.

Moby-Dick

Herman Melville

A monumental work of American literature, this novel chronicles the obsessive quest of Captain Ahab for revenge against the elusive white whale, Moby Dick. Through rich symbolism and philosophical musings, Melville explores themes of fate, obsession, and the human condition.

The Lord of the Rings

J.R.R. Tolkien

A beloved fantasy epic set in the fictional world of Middle-earth, where a young hobbit named Frodo Baggins embarks on a perilous journey to destroy the One Ring and defeat the dark lord Sauron. Along the way, he is joined by a diverse fellowship representing the various races of Middle-earth.

Charlotte BrontÃ«

A Gothic romance novel that follows the titular character, an orphaned governess, as she navigates love, morality, and social class in 19th-century England. Jane Eyre's journey of self-discovery and resilience captivates readers as she confronts the secrets of her past and fights for her independence.

The Odyssey

Homer

An ancient Greek epic poem that recounts the adventures of Odysseus as he strives to return home to Ithaca after the Trojan War. Filled with mythological creatures, heroic deeds, and divine interventions, "The Odyssey" is a timeless tale of perseverance, courage, and the longing for home.

The Guide

R.K. Narayan

This novel follows the life of a tour guide who rises to fame and fortune but finds himself entangled in moral and spiritual dilemmas. Set in Malgudi, a fictional South Indian town, R.K. Narayan's masterpiece explores themes of ambition, love, and redemption.

Wings of Fire: An Autobiography

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

In this inspirational autobiography, Dr. Kalam traces his journey from a small town in Tamil Nadu to becoming one of India's most renowned scientists and the 11th President of India. Through personal anecdotes and reflections, he shares the challenges he faced and the lessons he learned, emphasizing the importance of determination, hard work, and integrity.

Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

In this motivational book, Dr. Kalam urges the youth of India to dream big and work towards realizing their full potential. Drawing on his experiences and observations, he presents a vision for the future of the nation and calls for a collective effort to harness the talents and energies of its people for the greater good.

India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

In this visionary work, Dr. Kalam outlines his aspirations for India to emerge as a developed nation by the year 2020. He presents a roadmap for achieving economic growth, technological advancement, and social progress, emphasizing the importance of innovation, education, and inclusive development.

My Journey: Transforming Dreams into Actions

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

In this reflective memoir, Dr. Kalam shares the experiences and encounters that shaped his life and career. From his childhood dreams of flying to his contributions to India's space and defense programs, he recounts the highs and lows of his journey with humility and gratitude, highlighting the power of perseverance and passion in achieving one's goals.

Midnight's Children

Salman Rushdie

Winner of the Booker Prize, this novel tells the story of Saleem Sinai, a boy born at the stroke of midnight on the day of India's independence. Through Saleem's life, Rushdie weaves a magical and surreal tale that parallels the tumultuous history of post-colonial India.

The God of Small Things

Arundhati Roy

Set in Kerala, this Booker Prize-winning novel explores the lives of twins Rahel and Estha against the backdrop of family secrets, social taboos, and political unrest. Arundhati Roy's lyrical prose and poignant storytelling capture the complexities of love, loss, and identity.

A Suitable Boy

Vikram Seth

Set in post-independence India, this epic novel follows the lives of four families as they navigate love, politics, and tradition. Vikram Seth's sweeping narrative and richly drawn characters offer a panoramic view of Indian society during a time of great change.
